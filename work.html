Problem

Spotify gives users exactly what they want... almost to a fault. Spotify's algorithms focus on recommending music that users already know and like, rather than introducing them to new and exciting tracks. The platform also tends to overlook genres that users enjoy but don't often explore, limiting their ability to discover fresh tracks within their broader taste. In interviews, users expressed dissatisfaction with Spotify’s recommendations. While they appreciate how Spotify curates music to maintain a consistent vibe, many voiced a desire for more variety beyond their usual preferences.

Key Assumptions

1. Users want to store their favorite artists in one place while discovering new music based on their tastes.

2. The current algorithm limits users, preventing them from stepping outside their usual music bubble.

Outcome

One of the key design suggestions which I recommend is the introduction of a “Manage Algorithm” setting. This feature would allow users to customize their scope of recommendations which Spotify provides, giving users more control over their listening experience. The goal is to offer users the ability to adjust the algorithm’s reach, allowing users to explore music from genres they like but may not actively seek out, and beyond.

Interviews Conducted

Three Spotify users were interviewed.

User Pain Points

A common frustration voiced by users was that the algorithm has a tendency to recycle songs or deliver unsatisfactory recommendations. Many disliked the changes to how songs are added to the "Liked Songs" playlist, feeling it adds unnecessary steps. One interviewee voiced strong disapproval of Spotify's pre-curated playlists, which often they found duplicates their current favourite songs instead of introducing new music. Because of this, users opt to listen to user-generated playlists instead.

Engagement

Features like Spotify Wrapped and other engagement tools were viewed positively, enhancing the overall user experience. Interviewees expressed a desire for more human input in music recommendations, favoring personal curation, like playlists from friends, over algorithmic suggestions.

New Music vs. Variety

The algorithm tends to prioritize new music within the user’s current tastes, rather than offering diverse recommendations based on their past listening.

Users feel they aren't being introduced to truly new music, even though the suggestions align with their preferences. There is also a desire for variety across genres, not just new tracks within a specific genre. While some users appreciate personalized playlists like the Daily Mix or Discover Weekly, others reject them entirely.

Most users find algorithms useful for discovering new music, though success rates vary, and many prefer to discover music outside the platform, such as in social settings or through friends.

Problem

Spotify’s recommendations seem to fall between two primary parameters: relevance to vibe, genre, artist and familiarity to user. All interviewees have different music habits, but are all recommended music that is mostly familiar, at similar rates. This means that Spotify tends to emphasize music users already know, recommending less entirely “new” or “different” music. While this isn't inherently bad, as users may prefer the comfort of familiar music, but there is a potential issue: the algorithm may trap users in a feedback loop. They may not even realize they’re stuck or know how to break free and discover fresh, exciting music.

New Assumption

Because Spotify effectively delivers music that users like, many users are less inclined to actively search for new music. Instead, they rely on the platform’s recommendations, which reinforces the feedback loop and keeps users within their musical ecosystem.

Design Principles and Recommendations

Encourage Discovery: Offer more variety in recommendations. One of the best feelings when discovering new music is thinking, "I never thought I’d like this," which stems from novelty. The algorithm should strive to provide this type of experience.

Design Solution

The “Manage Algorithm” setting would work with three parameters: Newness, Variety, and Proximity. (These labels should be user-tested to ensure they are clear and intuitive.)

Manage Algorithm Design Statement Enhance Spotify's music discovery features by addressing frustrations with the algorithm's narrow focus. Improve the balance between personalized recommendations and exposure to new, diverse music. This can be achieved by refining Spotify’s algorithms, adjusting features, and promoting more community-driven content, ultimately improving user satisfaction.

Manage Algorithm Setting

Develop a new setting that allows users to customize the scope of their recommendations, enabling users to tailor their music feed to suit their preferences. The "Manage Algorithm" setting should align with the rest of Spotify’s settings, keeping the interface simple and intuitive. Given the concept's complexity, the design must be easy for users to understand and use. A visualizer will be essential to provide immediate feedback beyond just percentages. I propose a pill-shaped interface with three components: two orbs (green and purple) and an outline.

1. Green (Newness)

This controls how much new music a listener will hear. It adjusts the percentage of new songs versus familiar ones, particularly in situations like radio play or smart shuffle. The more users increases "newness," the more unfamiliar (new) music users will hear within their chosen vibe.

2. Purple (Variety)

This controls the variety of music a listener will hear. Spotify's current algorithm tends to restrict recommendations to genres that fit the user’s taste profile, leaving out genres they may also enjoy. The Variety parameter would allow users to expand (or narrow) their musical diet by adding or subtracting genres.

3. Outline (Proximity)

This parameter controls how close the recommendations stay to a user’s taste profile. Think of it as casting a net, but with adjustable size. The Proximity setting determines how far the algorithm will reach into different musical territories to provide suggestions, allowing users to explore music outside their usual preferences.

Wrap it up

By integrating these three components, the “Manage Algorithm” feature empowers users to take control of their music discovery experience and break free from repetitive and restricting recommendations.

It is fully within my knowledge that Spotify would never do this... but a feature such as this would be a first of its kind and would result in a content experience like no other.
